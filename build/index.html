Your DIY Questions Answered

How do I fix a leaky faucet?

Leaky faucets often stem from worn-out O-rings or cartridges. Turning off the water supply, disassembling the faucet, and replacing these parts usually solves the problem.

However, it is always best to call a professional plumber who can properly identify and fix the leak.

What's the best way to unclog a drain?

A plunger is often the first line of defense. If that fails, a drain snake can help remove deeper clogs. Chemical drain cleaners can be effective, but use them cautiously as they can damage pipes.

Calling a professional is always the best way to ensure your drain actually gets cleaned.

How do I fix a running toilet?

A running toilet usually indicates a problem with the flapper, chain, or fill valve. Inspecting these parts and replacing any worn or broken components can usually fix the issue.

A professional plumber will usually have all parts required for a fix on-hand.

How do I know if I have a leak in my pipes?

Signs of a leak include higher water bills, unexplained damp spots, or the sound of running water when no fixtures are in use. If you suspect you have a leak, it’s critical that you call a professional plumber to evaluate the situation.

Failure to act can lead to costly consequences.

Can I install a new toilet myself?

While possible, toilet installation requires some plumbing knowledge. We highly recommend that you call a professional plumber to do this kind of work.

DIY in this area can have costly outcomes.

How do I prevent frozen pipes in the winter?

Insulating exposed pipes, keeping the thermostat at a consistent temperature, and allowing faucets to drip slightly during freezing weather can help prevent frozen pipes.

What's the difference between PVC and copper pipes?

PVC pipes are plastic and generally used for drain lines, while copper pipes are metal and used for water supply lines. Copper is more durable but also more expensive.

What pipes to use is often dictated by building code, so we suggest consulting a professional plumber prior to pursuing a project.

When should I call a professional plumber?

If you're unsure about any DIY plumbing repair, or if the problem is complex or involves major components like the water heater or sewer line, it's always best to call a licensed plumber.

Metro Plumbing is always happy to help fix DIY mishaps, however, in an ideal world we can help avoid them entirely.
